## Recommended Additional IEEE/ACM Sources for AT4

### UK-Specific Healthcare Prediction
1. **Dantas et al. (2018)** - "No-shows in appointment scheduling" - OR Spectrum
   - Systematic review of no-show prediction methods across healthcare settings
   - DOI: 10.1007/s00291-018-0530-3

2. **Nelson et al. (2019)** - "Predicting scheduled hospital attendance with AI"
   - UK NHS hospital study, RF + gradient boosting
   - DOI: 10.1038/s41746-019-0103-3 (npj Digital Medicine)

3. **Dashtban and Li (2019)** - "Predicting non-attendance in hospital outpatient appointments"
   - UK NHS data, logistic regression baseline comparison
   - Published in Health Informatics Journal

4. **Harris et al. (2016)** - "Patient characteristics associated with non-attendance"
   - British Journal of General Practice, UK GP context directly relevant
   - DOI: 10.3399/bjgp16X686929

### Fairness and Bias in Healthcare ML
5. **Obermeyer et al. (2019)** - "Dissecting racial bias in an algorithm"
   - Science, landmark paper on healthcare algorithm bias
   - DOI: 10.1126/science.aax2342

6. **Chen et al. (2021)** - "Ethical ML in healthcare"
   - Annual Review of Biomedical Data Science
   - DOI: 10.1146/annurev-biodatasci-092820-114757

### Explainability in Clinical Decision Support
7. **Tonekaboni et al. (2019)** - "What clinicians want: contextualizing XAI for clinical end use"
   - ML4H workshop at NeurIPS, directly relevant to SHAP justification

8. **Ghassemi et al. (2021)** - "The false hope of current approaches to explainable AI in health"
   - The Lancet Digital Health, critical perspective on XAI limitations
   - DOI: 10.1016/S2589-7500(21)00208-9

### SMOTE and Class Imbalance
9. **Blagus and Lusa (2013)** - "SMOTE for high-dimensional class-imbalanced data"
   - BMC Bioinformatics, addresses SMOTE limitations relevant to Care Attend
   - DOI: 10.1186/1471-2105-14-106
